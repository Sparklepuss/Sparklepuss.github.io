Jojo Miin
I'm a lithuanian graphic designer with a passion for cinematography, microtypography, erotic expression, symbolism in design and the clash / unity of grid systems and chaotic imagery. I currently live in The Netherlands, where I received my bachelor’s degree in Graphic Design at the Royal Academy of Art.

In design, as in my personal life, I continuously strive to communicate as impactfully, passionately and pedantically the knowledge less understood, known or misinterpreted to those around me. The topics can be extensive and expansive, which I like to explore by implementing ordered structures to a whirlpool of interconnected information.

The "Western" Gods We Give Our Young

9min 40sec
1000x2000mm
1080p, 2022

In this project I try to illustrate, in a hyperbolic and slightly morbid way, the dehumanising, oppressive and discriminating memetic myths consistently repeated in video games, as they are currently the most dominant medium of communication in terms of use and profit. The social facts that we internalise while "praying" – devoting our time, money and emotions – undoubtably enter our everyday lives by what/how we act towards other people. We should not dismiss or fearmonger video games as the creators and only propagators of all modern evils – but neither should we accept the socially enforced worst part of them as norm, solely because someone once said it is "just a game".

2nd-LYF*

350x500mm, 2021

This is a newspaper print I made introducing the multiplicity and expansive possibility of the Second Life digital world experience. As Second Life is quite literally an environment to express your, well, second life: own a building that you rent, buy stocks, have a music career, create a loving family, all with real monetary or emotional connections to you, the person sitting in front of the screen.

I focus visually on connecting the digital and physical with a sanitary room you enter on each spread, filled with tangible, understandable items, 3D scanned in the real world, and partially, uncanny valley reaching, human elements of mismanaged proportions.

SAVAGE / CIVILISED

1min 59sec, 2019

A 3D 360 degree exploration of a “culture” totem dedicated to the narrative pillars of Aldous Huxley’s novel "Brave New World". Contrasting and comparing the so called “civilised” new society and the “savage” remnants of the old, I tried to bring out the absurd mimicry of the book through a single static speechless structure, all while the drastic and agressive values visualised of either side show just how equally oppressive they end up being.

Intersecting Colors
(redesign)

180x280mm, 2020

A few years ago I encountered the book "Intersecting Colors: Josef Albers and His Contemporaries" and was so intrigued by its base block nature, talking of impression and visual navigation, all while, at least in my opinion, losing the reader completely through the layout. After thinking about it for a little bit, I came to a curious question: what if I redesign it into a more compact, reader focussed, version of the book, but, just as the original, have a missing key feature - in this case, making it black and white, and see if the text about colours can manage to compensate for the lack of them.

Binck14

(with Aliz Soos), 2020-2022

A few years ago now my design partner Aliz Soos and I were chosen to work with "I’M BINCK", a collective of local company owners, artists and organisations, situated in the Binckhorst district of The Hague, to create a collective visual identity project, that would represent and connect each of the "BINCK14" locations.

We ended up developing 14 unique mural designs to be painted, hopefully within the upcoming years, on/of the buildings in each of these locations. The designs of each of them are supposed to connect all the areas together visually and create an individual, multiperspective representation of the surrounding architecture.

Tech-Uto /
Eco-So

4min 50sec, 2020

Tech-Uto/Eco-So is a video navigating an imaginary pavilion in the desert, dedicated to contemplating the techno-utopian and eco-soterian mindsets in the context of the so called "Anthropocene" (or, in simpler terms, what the hell do you chose to do now, as a human?), following a short message of reflection alongside it.

Inspired by the book Against the Anthropocene by T. J. Demos I wanted to recontextualise 3D scans of the, seemingly mundane and yet incredibly manufactured, street objects of The Hague within a completely natural desert setting, stitching the two together into, as Bruno Latour once called it, “a contemporary Frankenstein”.

XCVI CATS

70x120x35mm, 2020

While researching into modern values an inherent connection started to appear: that of humanity, its societal progress and the progress made in daily worship. And through extensive research into its different concepts the project’s focus of VALUE = WORSHIP (that religious/mythological/ mystical structures are a reflection of societal values at that time) was finally reached.

In this project I focused on translating my “value” research findings into an applicable postmodern form with roots in human history. I chose tarot cards, as they open up an opportunity for duality, as well as, a clear modern-day reflection to be later practically used.

absurdist MEMES

2min 36sec
180x280mm
A3, 2019

During my research into absurdist memes - punchline-less, set up-less deep-fried confusion, bringing out the darkest instinctual reflex of laughter for those so dopamine starved and bored with the formulaic, I started noticing an intriguing theory that they are a natural continuation and translation into the modern internet age of the famous post-Fluxus and neo-Dadaist movements.

Investigating this connection I developed a series of steps to creating my own versions of these memes and promptly made t-shirt designs, an advertisement video for them, a Riso printed poster series and a supplementary automated poster publication out of it.

Stuart Hall,
The Work of representation

200x270mm, 2019

During my bachelor’s studies I was introduced to this collection of texts when our teacher would hand out scanned printouts to take home and read every week. The topics and logical leaps in them were already hard enough to follow, not to mention that the density of the layout in the original book and the low quality of prints made it even more difficult.

For this exact reason, when deciding to redesign it, I chose to focus on how the reader navigates through each part of the text: would it be annotation, extra exercise, image or side note. The goal was to make a layout that would follow a natural eye path and the type of printing, paper and binding would make it quick to reproduce and easy to carry for everyone in the lecture.

1789:
Seminal Date, Person and Image

147x205mm, 2020

A small publication focussed on the crossection between the seminal date - 1789 (The Declaration of Human and Civic Rights by the National Constituent Assembly), the seminal person - Jacques Louis David (with my psychosocial reasonings of him, based on the Neo-Freudian psychologists theories: Alfred Adler’s “inferiority complex”, Erik Erikson’s “psychosocial stages of development”, Carl Jung’s “persona theory” and Karen Horney’s “prepubescent coping styles”), and the seminal image - Jacques-Louis David’s “Tennis Court Oath”. All of which are shown within a deeply pedantic design, pointing out every connection and reference within.

Rendition of the Repurposed and Restructured

240x305mm, 2021

After years of teachers and country leaders explaining how we are every day inching closer to every type of dystopia, which is not just our possible but clearly our inevitable future, and how simply from the core origins and meaning of the word, utopia is completely imaginary and unattainable to anyone, I felt really annoyed by such a lazy defeatist approach to accept the worst because: “oh, no, it was always bad, and always will be, so I don’t have to care or try”. And from one such specific encounter, followed by annoyance fueled research, came a simple answer to an, in their mind "clearly impossible”, ecological question. Algae.

Algae everywhere in our industrial architecture for air filtering, heating, aesthetics, fuel and even food. This answer I made into a self explanatory utopian photography book.

POSTAL Fuxus

A1, 2020

When quarantining had just started in the Netherlands and the feelings of isolation began hitting everyone all at once, our screens became a very effective, of course, but also a really cold and distant solution to staying in touch and seeing each other.

In response to this situation I decided to make the dumbest, most cringe meme culture-y, and relaxing personal postcards I could draw, by stealing, remaking, recontextualising and purging my brain of all of the intrusive, confusing and overwhelming thoughts as jokes I had. I did so in the hopes that the friends I send them out to would relate to how I feel, would just enjoy getting a little gift or simply laugh at how dumb it all is.

Against the Anthropocene
(emphasised by Hayao Miyazaki)

140x215mm, 2021

While delving deeper into the texts of T. J. Demos’ - Against the Anthropocene: Visual Culture and Environment Today, I kept having these beautifully animated memories pop up, as if certain arguments connected with a visual metaphor I once saw. Only after randomly desiding on a movie to watch, did I realise what deeply philosophical ecological questions Hayao Miyazaki poses in his films. I redesigned the original book with a strong, almost overpowering focus on the references, annotations and secondary research, each separate essay being echoed by a Studio Ghibli movie, each argument within the pages connected with a quote and the timestamp of it.
